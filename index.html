MS by Research, Robotics Research Center, IIIT Hyderabad

Amitabh Sharma

Adaptive and impedance control for aerial manipulators that have to interact with the world, with a particular interest in safety filters, payload-aware controllers, and proofs that can be machine-checked.

Featured work

Payload Feasibility Certificate

A pre-flight test that asks whether an aerial manipulator can safely lift a given payload, with a closed-form bound and a Lean 4 proof script.

Adaptive Impedance for Aerial Catching

A 2R arm on a hovering quadrotor catches a thrown ball, estimating the payload mass on impact while keeping the wrench delivered back to the base under control.

Research interests

⟁

Adaptive impedance control

Compliant aerial manipulation under unknown vehicle–arm coupling. Stability via Lyapunov; experiments on real hardware.

◇

Safety filters with certificates

Energy-based barrier functions paired with closed-form pre-flight bounds, plus Lean 4 verification of the load-bearing lemmas.

◯

Fault-tolerant flight

Quadrotor control under partial actuator loss and state-dependent disturbances, with adaptive laws that survive the failure mode.
